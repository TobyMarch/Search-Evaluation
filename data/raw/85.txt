<p><b>Mufasa</b>

He

He

<p><b>Rafiki</b>
is
a
<a
href="/wiki/Mandrill?qsrc=3044"
title="Mandrill">mandrill</a>
who
lives
in
a
<a
href="/wiki/Baobab?qsrc=3044"
title="Baobab">baobab</a>
tree
somewhere
in
the
Pride
Lands.
He
is
a
dear
friend
to
Mufasa
and
later
Simba.
He
performs
<a
href="/wiki/Shamanism?qsrc=3044"
title="Shamanism">shamanistic</a>
services
for
the
lions
of
Pride
Rock.
He
is
an
enigmatic
character,
tending
to
speak
about
himself
in
the
third
person,
who
drops
in
unexpectedly
to
lend
advice;
although
very
wise,
he
also
has
a
very
quirky,
playful
side.
As
a
bipedal
animal,
Rafiki
is
able
to
use
props
more
easily
than
most
of
the
films'
animals.
He
is
never
seen
without
his
stick,
which
is
topped
with
<a
href="/wiki/Gourd?qsrc=3044"
title="Gourd">gourds</a>
that
he
uses
for
ritual
purposes,
painting
and
food.
The
character
often
serves
as
the
narrator
of
the
story
of
<i>The
Lion
King</i>,
especially
in
video
games
and
merchandise.
Rafiki's
name
means
&quot;friend&quot;
in
Swahili.</p>








<p>In
<i>The
Lion
King</i>,
Rafiki
is
introduced
in
the
opening
scene
when
he
travels
to
Pride
Rock
to
perform

Simba's
presentation

Mufasa

has
not
seen
in
a
long
time.
Rafiki
anoints
Simba
and
presents
him
to
the
gathered
animals,
and
later
draws
a

lion
cub
on
the
walls
of
his
treehouse
home
to

Simba's
birth.
When
Simba
is
believed
to
be
dead,
Rafiki
draws
his
hand

the
Simba

Later,

his

on
the
dust

air,
Rafiki

He

to

and

He

Simba
that
his
father
Mufasa

in

the
appearance
of
Mufasa's

which

Simba
the

to
face
his

During

Pride
Rock,
Rafiki
displays
highly
trained

arts

hyenas
and
save

hyena
who
attack
him
and

was
Scar

Simba
embraces

ascending
Pride
Rock

&quot;It

<p>During

<p>Scar

glad
to
see
Timon
go.

appears
again
towards
the
end
of

film,

and
Ma
encounter
Timon
and
Pumbaa
at
Pride
Rock,
and
helps
Timon
get
rid
of

finally

chi</a>

be
eaten.</p>
