<h2><span

<p>In
1979,
Madonna
starred
in
low-budget
feature
<i><a
href="/wiki/A_Certain_Sacrifice?qsrc=3044"
title="A
Certain
Sacrifice">A
Certain
Sacrifice</a></i>.
Its
release
in
1985
coincided
with
the
success
of
her
<i>Like
a
Virgin</i>
album,
and
Madonna
tried
to
prevent
its
release.
That
same
year,
Madonna
made
a
small
cameo
appearance
as
a
club
singer
in
the
film
<i><a
href="/wiki/Vision_Quest?qsrc=3044"
title="Vision
Quest">Vision
Quest</a></i>
singing
<i>Crazy
for
You</i>;
then
garnered
commercial
and
critical
success
in
<a
href="/wiki/Susan_Seidelman?qsrc=3044"
title="Susan
Seidelman">Susan
Seidelman</a>'s
<i><a
href="/wiki/Desperately_Seeking_Susan?qsrc=3044"
title="Desperately
Seeking
Susan">Desperately
Seeking
Susan</a></i>,
a
story
of
a
housewife
who
is
fascinated
with
a
woman
she
only
knows
by
reading
messages
in
the
personals
section
of
a
<a
href="/wiki/New_York_City?qsrc=3044"
title="New
York
City">New
York
City</a>
tabloid.
It
was
a
commercial
success
and
grossed
$27
million
in
the
United
States
alone.<sup
id="cite_ref-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup></p>








<p>Madonna
then
appeared
in
the
adventure
drama
<i><a
href="/wiki/Shanghai_Surprise?qsrc=3044"
title="Shanghai
Surprise">Shanghai
Surprise</a></i>
(1986)
with
then-husband
<a
href="/wiki/Sean_Penn?qsrc=3044"
title="Sean
Penn">Sean
Penn</a>.
The
film
was
dismissed
by
moviegoers
and
received
poor
reviews,
many
of
them
calling
her
acting
wooden.
Subsequent
films
such
as
<i><a
href="/wiki/Who%27s_That_Girl_(1987_film)?qsrc=3044"
title="Who's
That
Girl
(1987
film)">Who's
That
Girl</a></i>
(1987)

<i><a
href="/wiki/Bloodhounds_of_Broadway_(1989_film)?qsrc=3044"

Broadway
(1989

of
Broadway</a></i>
(1989)
failed
to
attract
commercial
or
critical
success.
She
was
the
first
choice
for
the
role
of
&quot;Susie
Diamond&quot;
in
<i><a
href="/wiki/The_Fabulous_Baker_Boys?qsrc=3044"
title="The
Fabulous
Baker
Boys">The
Fabulous
Baker
Boys</a></i>,
but
famously
rejected
the
part
claiming
it
was
&quot;too
mushy&quot;.<sup
id="cite_ref-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup>
This
proved
to
be
an
unwise
decision
on
her
part
because
the
film
turned
out
to
be
a
box-office
success
and
it
brought
<a
href="/wiki/Michelle_Pfeiffer?qsrc=3044"
title="Michelle
Pfeiffer">Michelle
Pfeiffer</a>
to
even
greater
fame.</p>








<p>In

good
reviews
for

<i><a

<a

<a

Gould">Chester

<a

strip">comic
strip</a>.

<i><a
href="/wiki/Madonna:_Truth_or_Dare?qsrc=3044"

<a

<i><a
href="/wiki/Boxing_Helena?qsrc=3044"

<h2><span
class="mw-headline"
id="Documentaries">Documentaries</span></h2>








<p>In
1991,
Madonna
released
her
first
documentary
<i><a
href="/wiki/Madonna:_Truth_or_Dare?qsrc=3044"
title="Madonna:
Truth
or
Dare">Madonna:
Truth
or
Dare</a></i>
(named
<i><a
href="/wiki/Madonna:_Truth_or_Dare?qsrc=3044"
title="Madonna:
Truth
or
Dare">In
Bed
With
Madonna</a></i>
outside
North
America).
Directed
by
<a
href="/wiki/Alek_Keshishian?qsrc=3044"
title="Alek
Keshishian">Alek
Keshishian</a>,
the
film
followed
Madonna
on
her
1990
<a
href="/wiki/Blond_Ambition_World_Tour?qsrc=3044"
title="Blond
Ambition
World
Tour">Blond
Ambition
World
Tour</a>.
It
featured
black
and
white
backstage
scenes
and
live
performances
filmed
in
color.
The
documentary
was
released
in
North
America
by
<a
href="/wiki/Miramax_Films?qsrc=3044"
title="Miramax
Films">Miramax
Films</a>
and
grossed
more
than
$15
million
in
the
U.S.
alone.<sup
id="cite_ref-12"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-12">[13]</a></sup>
While
criticized
for
being
manufactured,
the
film
offered
insights
into
Madonna's
relationship
with
then-boyfriend
<a
href="/wiki/Warren_Beatty?qsrc=3044"
title="Warren
Beatty">Warren
Beatty</a>
and
showed
her
admitting
that
ex-husband
<a
href="/wiki/Sean_Penn?qsrc=3044"
title="Sean
Penn">Sean
Penn</a>
was
the
love
of
her
life.
Madonna
deeply
dislikes
the
title
of
<i>In
Bed
With
Madonna</i>
and
has
expressed
in
interviews
that
it
is
a
&quot;stupid
title&quot;.
The
original
title
of
the
documentary
was
<i>&quot;Truth
or
Dare:
On
The
Road,
Behind
The
Scenes
and
In
Bed
With
Madonna&quot;</i>.</p>








<p>In
1992,
behind
the
scenes
footage
was
filmed
by
<a
href="/wiki/Fabien_Baron?qsrc=3044"
title="Fabien
Baron">Fabien
Baron</a>
during
the
making
of
the
book
<i><a
href="/wiki/Sex_(book)?qsrc=3044"
title="Sex
(book)">Sex</a></i>.
This
footage
was
shown
at
the
launch
party
of
the
book,
and
has
since
been
leaking
on
the
internet.
It
includes
many
of
the
images
used
in
the
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Erotica_(song)?qsrc=3044"
title="Erotica
(song)">Erotica</a>&quot;
music
video
accompanied
by
1920s
music.
It
was
handed
out
to
100
special
guests
at
the
launch
and
is
listed
on
<a
href="/wiki/Internet_Movie_Database?qsrc=3044"
title="Internet
Movie
Database">www.imdb.com</a>
as
a
documentary.
It
is
sometimes
known
as
<i>The
Making
of
Sex</i>.</p>








<p>In
2005,
a
short
internet
documentary
<i><a
href="/wiki/Confessions_on_a_Dance_Floor?qsrc=3044#Promotion"
title="Confessions
on
a
Dance
Floor">Confessions
on
a
Promo
Tour</a></i>
was
created
to
promote
the
release
of
the
album
<i><a
href="/wiki/Confessions_on_a_Dance_Floor?qsrc=3044"
title="Confessions
on
a
Dance
Floor">Confessions
on
a
Dance
Floor</a></i>.
It
featured
behind-the-scenes
footage
of
the
rehearsals
for
the
<a
href="/wiki/List_of_Madonna_concert_tours?qsrc=3044#Promotional_tours"
title="List
of
Madonna
concert
tours">Hung
Up
Promo
Tour</a>.
The
documentary
was
produced
by
<a
href="/wiki/HSI?qsrc=3044"
title="HSI">HSI</a><sup
class="plainlinks
noprint
Inline-Template"
style="vertical-align:text-top;white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a
href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tools:%7Edispenser/cgi-bin/dab_solver.py&amp;page=Madonna_filmography&amp;editintro=Template:Disambiguation_needed/editintro&amp;client=Template:Dn"
class="external
text"
rel="nofollow"><span
title="Link
needs
disambiguation
from
January
2012">disambiguation
needed</span></a></i>]</sup>,
Exposure
Films
and
Cheeky
Boy
Films.
It
was
directed
by
Dan
Cadan,
produced
by
Melissa
Thomas
and
executive
produced
by
Nicola
Doring.</p>








<p>Later
that
year,
Madonna
released
her
second
full-length
documentary,
<i><a
href="/wiki/I%27m_Going_to_Tell_You_a_Secret?qsrc=3044"
title="I'm
Going
to
Tell
You
a
Secret">I'm
Going
to
Tell
You
a
Secret</a></i>,
followed
her
and
her
family
on

2004
<a
href="/wiki/Re-Invention_World_Tour?qsrc=3044"
title="Re-Invention
World
Tour">Re-Invention
World
Tour</a>
in

Directed
by
long-time
collaborator
<a
href="/wiki/Jonas_%C3%85kerlund?qsrc=3044"
title="Jonas
&Aring;kerlund">Jonas
&Aring;kerlund</a>,
it
premiered
commercial
free
on
<a
href="/wiki/MTV?qsrc=3044"
title="MTV">MTV</a>
in
the
<a
href="/wiki/United_States?qsrc=3044"
title="United
States">US</a>
on
October
21,
2005.
It
was
released
on
DVD
June
20,
2006
with
a
bonus
audio
CD.</p>








<p>In
2006,
a
second
short
internet
documentary
<i><a
href="/wiki/Confessions_Tour?qsrc=3044"
title="Confessions
Tour">Confessions
Tour:
Behind
the
Scenes</a></i>
was
created
for
the
<a
href="/wiki/Confessions_Tour?qsrc=3044"
title="Confessions
Tour">Confessions
Tour</a>
website.
It
featured
behind-the-scenes
footage
from
the
tour
and
rehearsals.
The
film
was
later
released
as
an
extra
on
the
2007
DVD
and
live
album
release
<i><a
href="/wiki/The_Confessions_Tour_(album)?qsrc=3044"
title="The
Confessions
Tour
(album)">The
Confessions
Tour</a></i>.</p>








<p>Later
that
year,
Madonna
announced
that
she
was
funding
a
documentary
about
Malawi
and
it
was
through
viewing
this
footage
that
she
first
saw
David
Banda,
whom
she
has
since
adopted.
The
film
is
called
<i><a
href="/wiki/I_Am_Because_We_Are?qsrc=3044"
title="I
Am
Because
We
Are">I
Am
Because
We
Are</a></i>
and
was
released
in
2008,
directed
by
Nathan
Rissman
and
produced,
narrated
and
starring
Madonna.</p>








<p>In
2010,
Madonna
released
the
DVD
and
live
album
<i><a
href="/wiki/Sticky_%26_Sweet_Tour_(album)?qsrc=3044"
title="Sticky
&amp;

(album)">Sticky
&amp;
Sweet
Tour</a></i>
which

the
bonus
feature,
<i><a

&amp;
Sweet

&amp;

World

-

the
Scenes</a></i>,
a

documentary

on
the
<a
href="/wiki/Sticky_%26_Sweet_Tour?qsrc=3044"
title="Sticky
&amp;
Sweet
Tour">Sticky
&amp;
Sweet
Tour</a>
and
included
rehearsals
and
backstage
footage,

scenes
with
<a
href="/wiki/Pharrell_Williams?qsrc=3044"
title="Pharrell
Williams">Pharrell
Williams</a>,
<a

title="Justin
Timberlake">Justin
Timberlake</a>
and
<a
href="/wiki/Timbaland?qsrc=3044"
title="Timbaland">Timbaland</a>
on
stage.
The
short
was
directed
by
Nathan
Rissman.</p>
