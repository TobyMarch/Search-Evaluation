<p><i><b>ABBA</b></i>
is
the
eponymously-titled
third
studio
album
by
the
Swedish
pop
group
<a
href="/wiki/ABBA?qsrc=3044"
title="ABBA">ABBA</a>.
It
was
originally
released
on
21
April
1975
through
<a
href="/wiki/Polar_Music?qsrc=3044"
title="Polar
Music">Polar
Music</a>.</p>








<p>Following
the
<a
href="/wiki/Eurovision_Song_Contest?qsrc=3044"
title="Eurovision
Song
Contest">Eurovision</a>
success
of
&quot;Waterloo&quot;,
<i>ABBA</i>
saw
the
band
gaining
worldwide
recognition.
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/I_Do,_I_Do,_I_Do,_I_Do,_I_Do?qsrc=3044"
title="I
Do,
I
Do,
I
Do,
I
Do,
I
Do">I
Do,
I
Do,
I
Do,
I
Do,
I
Do</a>&quot;
topped
the
charts
in
Australia,
as
did
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/Mamma_Mia_(song)?qsrc=3044"
title="Mamma
Mia
(song)">Mamma
Mia</a>&quot;
shortly
after.
&quot;<a
href="/wiki/SOS_(ABBA_song)?qsrc=3044"
title="SOS
(ABBA
song)">SOS</a>&quot;
and
&quot;Mamma
Mia&quot;
became
hits
in
both
the
United
States
and
the
United
Kingdom,
and
two
of
its
most
famous
songs.
The
album
saw
ABBA
dabble
into
reggae
with
&quot;Tropical
Loveland&quot;
and
includes
a
grand
pseudo-classical
keyboard
instrumental
in
the
traditions
of
<a
href="/wiki/Keith_Emerson?qsrc=3044"
title="Keith
Emerson">Keith
Emerson</a>
and
<a
href="/wiki/Rick_Wakeman?qsrc=3044"
title="Rick
Wakeman">Rick
Wakeman</a>
with
&quot;Intermezzo
No.
1&quot;
(originally
entitled
&quot;Mama&quot;).<sup
id="cite_ref-0"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup></p>








<p><i>ABBA</i>
was
first
released
on
CD
in
Japan
in
1986,
with
an
alternative
mix
of
&quot;Man
in
the
Middle&quot;
not
found
on
any
subsequent
CD
pressing.<sup
id="cite_ref-1"
class="reference"><a
href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup>
&quot;
<i>ABBA</i>
was
then
released
on
CD
in
West
Germany
in
1987
(later
released
internationally),
with
five
bonus
tracks
of
songs
from
<i><a
href="/wiki/Waterloo_(album)?qsrc=3044"
title="Waterloo
(album)">Waterloo</a></i>
and
<i><a
href="/wiki/Ring_Ring_(album)?qsrc=3044"
title="Ring
Ring
(album)">Ring
Ring</a></i>,
which
had
not
been
released
on
CD
at
that
time.
<i>ABBA</i>
was
released
on
CD
in
Sweden
in
1988
featuring
the
original
11
tracks
only.
The
album
has
been
reissued
in
digitally
remastered
form
three
times;
first
in
1997,
then
in
2001
and
again
in
2005
as
part
of
<i><a
href="/wiki/The_Complete_Studio_Recordings_(ABBA_album)?qsrc=3044"
title="The
Complete
Studio
Recordings
(ABBA
album)">The
Complete
Studio
Recordings</a></i>
<a
href="/wiki/Box_set?qsrc=3044"
title="Box
set">box
set</a>.</p>
