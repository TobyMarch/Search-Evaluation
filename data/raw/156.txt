<h1>Elvis
Presley
&amp;
Madonna:
The
Day
the
Music
Died,
and
Was
Born</h1>









<div
class="subtitle">










by
Keith
Caulfield
&amp;
Gary
Trust&nbsp;&nbsp;|&nbsp;&nbsp;
August
16,
2012
12:05
EDT









</div>









<div
class="main-article">










<p>Today
marks
the
date
that
we
welcomed
the
Queen
of
Pop
and
lost
the
King
of
Rock
&amp;
Roll.
<br
/>
<br
/>
<a
href="artist/madonna/50294">Madonna</a>
was
born
Aug.
16,
1958.
As
we
celebrate
the
Material
Girl's
54th
birthday,
we
likewise
note
the
35th
anniversary
of
the
passing
of
<a
href="artist/elvis-presley/5444">Elvis
Presley</a>,
who
died
on
the
same
date
in
1977
at
age
42.
<br
/>
<br
/>
In
honor
of
two
of
the
most
prolific
hitmakers
in
Billboard
history,
here
is
a
look
at
some
of
the
chart
records
that
the
icons
claim.
<br
/>
<br
/>
<br
/>
<strong>MADONNA
<br
/>
</strong></p>










<p>-
<strong>38
top
10
Billboard
Hot
100
singles</strong>.
The
<a
href="artist/the-beatles/4057">Beatles</a>,
with
34,
rank
second.
Madonna's
first
top
10
was
1984's
&quot;Borderline,&quot;
which
started
a
streak
of
17
consecutive
top
10s
through
1989's
&quot;Cherish&quot;
(No.
2).
Her
most
recent
top
10
was
this
year's
No.
10-peaking
&quot;Give
Me
All
Your
Luvin',&quot;
featuring
<a
href="artist/nicki-minaj/998160">Nicki
Minaj</a>
and
M.I.A.</p>










<p>&nbsp;</p>










<p
style="text-align:
center;">
<a
href="features/madonna-a-ron-galella-photo-celebration-1004109343.story">
<strong>Madonna:
A
Ron
Galella
Photo
Celebration</strong></a>
</p>










<p>&nbsp;</p>










<p>-
<strong>56
titles
on
the
Hot
100
since
her
debut</strong>
the
week
of
Oct.
29,
1983,
with
&quot;Holiday,&quot;
which
peaked
at
No.
16
in
January
1984.
Since
her
Hot
100
arrival,
Madonna
leads
all
women
for
the
most
chart
appearances.
<a
href="artist/taylor-swift/766101">Taylor
Swift</a>
<a
href="artist/mariah-carey/48340"></a>
ranks
second
with
45
visits
in
that
span,
followed
by
<a
href="artist/mariah-carey/48340">Mariah
Carey</a>
(44),
Minaj
(41)
and
<a
href="artist/mary-j-blige/43746">Mary
J.
Blige</a>
(40).
<br
/>
<br
/>
<br
/>
-
<strong>Seven
Hot
100
No.
1s
in
the
'80s</strong>,
tying
her
with
Whitney
Houston
for
most
among
women
during
the
decade.
Madonna's
total
haul
of
No.
1
Hot
100
hits
is
12,
the
fourth-most
leaders
all-time.
(The
Supremes
also
boast
12
toppers).
Only
the
Beatles
(with
20),
Carey
(18)
and
<a
href="artist/michael-jackson/4902">Michael
Jackson</a>
(13)
have
earned
more
No.
1s.
Coincidentally,
Madonna
claimed
her
fourth
No.
1,
&quot;Papa
Don't
Preach,&quot;
on
this
date
in
1986,
her
28th
birthday.
(Aug.
16
also
doubles
as
the
launch
date
of
Billboard's
Dance
Airplay
chart
in
2003,
a
list
that
Madonna
has
led
seven
times).
<br
/>

/>

<p>&nbsp;</p>










<p>
<br

-
<strong>42

Dance/Club

chart</strong>.
Essentially,
if
Madonna
releases
a
single
and
it
charts
on
Dance/Club
Play
Songs,

a
safe
bet
to
assume
it'll
go
to
No.
1.
Since
2000,
she's
placed
26
hits
on
the
survey.
Of

&quot;Turn

to

No.

fourth
frame
this
week.
Madonna
is
clearly
the
Queen
of
the
Club.
(
<a
href="artist/janet-jackson/4899">Janet
Jackson</a>
is
next
with
19
No.
1s).</p>










<p>&nbsp;</p>










<p
style="text-align:
center;">
<a

10

<p>-
<strong>20
top
10
albums
on
the
Billboard
200,
the
most
such
sets
among
all
artists
dating
to
her
first
week
in
the
top
tier</strong>
(Oct.
6,
1984)
with
her
self-titled
debut.
Of
those
efforts,
eight
reached
No.
1,
including
&quot;MDNA&quot;
in
April.
In
that
28-year
span,
<a
href="artist/george-strait/2521">George
Strait</a>
places
second
with
17
top
10s,
followed
by
Carey
(16).
<br
/>
<br
/>
<br
/>
<br
/>
<strong>ELVIS
PRESLEY</strong>
<br
/>
<br
/>
-
<strong>108
Hot
100
hits,
the
most
among
solo
artists</strong>
in
the

history
(among
all
acts,
only
the
<a

cast
(203)
has
inked
more
charted
hits;
Presley's
career
predates
the
Hot
100's
1958
inception).
The
King's
104th
charted
title,
&quot;Way
Down,&quot;
originally
peaked
at
No.
31
the
weeks
of
Aug.
6
and
13,
1977.
After
dipping
to
No.
53,
following
Presley's
death,
the
song
roared
35-24-21-18,
marking
his
last
top
20
track.</p>










<p>&nbsp;</p>










<p
style="text-align:
center;">

</p>










<p

<a

Hot
100,
dated
Aug.
4,
1958,
Presley
ranked
at
No.
4
with
&quot;Hard
Headed

No.
34

&quot;Don't

longtime
gospel

band,
the
Jordanaires.
<br
/>
<br
/>
<br
/>
-
<strong>124

albums</strong>,
the
highest

in
the

56-year

No.
1,

&quot;Elvis
Presley,&quot;

His

&quot;Elv1s:
30
#1
Hits&quot;

Just

at
No.
137
with
&quot;I
Am
an
Elvis

/>

/>

<p>-

<p>&nbsp;</p>










<p
style="text-align:
center;">

<p>&nbsp;</p>










<p>-
Presley

the
<strong>first
rock
artist
to

to
the
Hot
100
summit</strong>,
as
&quot;Stuck
